I gotta say that I was fairly let down by Samsung, which introduced a product that I just did not believe to be as compelling as it ought to. After having switched back to S7, I discovered how great of a product they actually managed to release.

First of all, which hit me once I started using Galaxy S7 was how ergonomic it is. Basically I took the product into my hand and automatically realized that it's just so convenient to carry it, that it's almost insane. Furthermore even the way you touch the product differs from the Note 3 I had and used to praise.

Secondly, how fast it is. Basically any interaction is instant. You want left? It goes even faster than how you interact with it. Everything goes instantly.


...


Camera is significantly better and takes pictures significantly faster.

The technologies are the deal breaker as far as I'm concerned. I wish I had an Iris scanner embedded but...

There are multiple ways of recovering your data. At first I did a smart switch from my note 3. This time I just recovered most of my data through Samsung, except for the pictures and such. Still, 15 GB on a cloud? That's quite solid. And you still may use a different Cloud option.

It turns out that you may get information regarding what's nearby if your bluetooth is enabled. Basic information, website etc. Manage passwords to all your accounts.

Use assistants out of which your phone may notify you once it hears a crying baby (!) or a ring on your door (!!). And then use your flash (!!!) to notify you!

Send an SOS recording 5 seconds sound and pictures from the back and front once you hit your power button 3 times.

Log in to applications and web application with your fingerprint (biometrics!). Tell your phone when not to bother you (by default 22-7). You may even change the PIN to your sim (?o.O(

The phone may also check whether you have the phone with you. In this case it will not trigger the fingerprint thing (if you're underway). You may add known places such as your home in which the same will happen.

After a few questions asked, actually I just said that it was bought a year ago, that it was bought in Poland and it was recently serviced in Poland. The seller was after a document that showcased that it was serviced in a proper place. Unfortunately I forgot that I did not top my alditalk account up yet, so I voiced my concern that I could go to the uni and check for the documents. I mentioned samsung guard and then basically the guy started filling a paper straightaway. Actually, he even forced the device open, but then I allowed him to.

He even offered me a job, as he said that they are always looking for students who could fill some places in. I explained that I am an exchange student and politely replied that it is my last month on exchange, therefore it would be relatively difficult. Then he asked that I could perhaps know some people who would be interested in finding a job there and I said that I might know a few people so I'll let them know.

I asked me to introduce the details into the system (because of my name). I introduced the details. Then I got a form to sign. Before that I also asked if the repair is covered by the warranty.
