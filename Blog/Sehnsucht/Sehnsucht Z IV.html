Although, I gotta say that I'm quite disappointed and the reason for that is quite strictly the following. I really did actually learn. I made the effort of attending all of the lectures and even did extra work for the tutorials. It is true that I dropped ball on one lecture, mistakenly dodging a few of the tutorials and not making sure that I actually completed the exercises. And for some of the lectures I turned up after a rough night of semi-partying, but despite these excesses I did actually listen and watch the lectures. Moreover I rewatched all of them, one of 'em even twice in a row. And once the examination period started, I annotated a big chunk of the slides. I read them, learned them, even spoke them to the microphone so that I could listen to them afterwards.

Critical Dillema

I did not do it strictly at home. I tried at a reading room, a library, didn't really work that well, tho, too many people for my taste and too distracting. So at home I annotated on my bed. I annotated by my desk and on my chair. I annotated them outside on my balcony, having my liter of tea by my side and a cup of brew in my palm. With another, annotating. I annotated at 8 am. At 8 pm. By the sunset and sunrise. I learned for hours and did nothing for hours as well but I made the effort. I even annotated them in a bus, I annotated them in a train. I annotated them at my exchange university. I annotated them at another universities (Geneva for example). I listened to them along my path and did my best to learn it in great lengths. But for fucks' sake, 737 pages of slides to be comprehended?

And I've got some other ideas, losing my mind is one of 'em already in motion. Having sleepless nights, easy peasy. How about meeting some people in the meantime, trying to make my brain move and work at last. Get out of my marasmus, isolationist malaise. And try leaving a bit, beyond my trips. Try actually liking what I'm learning and willing to understand it, figuring how it might fit my area of specialization quite nicely. Picture the world alternatively, perceive the another. Shake it, chuck it, put it upside down. Let me take another gigantic leap forward and put some sacrifices on the table, (because and) for what's it worth.
