So, what's so fantastic about this show? I always found value in the multitude of references to our lives. Artificial Intelligence and the dangers associated with it. The way people think and the verges of our humanity. Embroided were lines associated with IT, philosophy, literature. The scaling of the world, starting from people taking part in this game, moving on towards the organisation which manages it all. Actors' performance of superior quality with Anthony Hopkins being part of the cast. Sound with the leading motives, which may be called classics already. The use of a myriad of motifs, techniques. The most important from all this is the following: the focus on an entire spectrum of captured real-world issues and driving them in a subtle way, leaving the plot complexities at the backseat, while making sure it all continues without any plot-related errors. A show with a potential for which I was waiting a long time, which brings a really refreshing air breeze.

Meanwhile, Black Mirror. I won't write about the original seasons, as it could do with a few A4 pages to elaborate on what makes it what it makes, my favourite and imho genius show. Emotions are a funny thing. We can launch them easily, yet at times its a fickle to put them aside. Despite it all, everyone may value a title, which realistically evokes strong emotions, even resonates so heavily that you'll begin to cry. Or a human will fall into deep anger. Or even fall in love. And there are many different emotions, which a title can evoke, yet I suspect it's all about those higher ones, about which I do not really know honestly. This magic, which releases something in a person, which the title itself clusters out with imagination and execution. Unfortunately, the new Black Mirror let me down, although it had its moments and potential, which remained underutilised throughout most of its time.

First episode: great theme, down to earth and current. Really interesting extra themes, for instance the brother of the main character, who's as if the level-headed version of the protagonist. A few interesting comparisons. Yet the episode detached eventually and lead towards nowhere. It ended up weak, leaving the audience without much. A few interesting quotes. A massive, gigantic potential which went straight to the bin.

Second episode, phenomenal start. Great approach to the way the leading character was built in 15 minutes, the background, motivates and overall, absolute masterpiece. Phenomenal performance, especially of the leading character. Then it starts to become a bit cliched, although quality level remained well. Afterwards, things happened, very interesting things, although very badly concluded inception. I'd rather say it was a rather good episode, although one shouldn't wrap up such phenomenal episodes in such way. Plus one scene completely took me apart. But it wasn't enough.

Fifth episode... Again, underutilised potential. Well lead but nothing extraordinatory wasn't included. To the point. The ending started to become quite interesting. Dystopian future, heh. Almost, as one powerful scene does not alter the perception of all the rest, which the viewer has experienced. That was the case in here. Classic, untapped potential.

The sixth episode was the classic black mirror. 1.5h show, created as a conspiracy-thing, which feels a bit cliche but not quite in here. It's hard to explain without providing many spoilers. But as I disliked all the previous episodes, usually due to the technology not being the frame holoding it all and being focused on, without the frame of which the plotlines wouldn't have much to lay against. Here it was a strong draw to the technological aspect in a way that could reflect on our own civilisation. The episode provides a massive kick, it's directed well and quite well written as well. Although the ending... I felt like ripping Charlie Brooker's balls out for what he'd done, only a dick would be able to finish up an episode in such a mediocre way. Well.. There's still Westworld.
