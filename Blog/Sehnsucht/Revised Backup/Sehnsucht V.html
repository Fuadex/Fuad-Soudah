Sehnsucht X


I'm sitting now at 4 o'clock in the morning, woken up by loosely lead conversations from students a few blocks down. It's hot but usually in the morning it can be really chilly. Despite this, I keep my doors wide open, because if I have to choose between waking up and living in a tightly sealed room, I prefer the better first option. 'Tis merely I get to be a bit more alive and figuring. A consistent flow of air is the basis and this bungalow in which I normally am is like a bunker! They were building this village during the cold war, so perhaps that's the reason?

The last 1.5 weeks were abundant. After previous posts you could deduct what sort of a brothel (yyy, I meant mess) I have in life, I lack routine, a bit of figuring myself out. And despite this, I managed by far to sort out lots of stuff, although it's still relatively not enough. In which, I had many things left for later, due to which lots continuously accumulate into a pack that continuously fade away in my eyes, not disappearing altogether, yet remaining on the horizon. For instance, I'm writing a few important emails for a few good months (to be honest, I'm not sure what these emails were at the time. Could've been to my lecturers back in Sydney, I had a thing to write extensive emails but a few months? damn). And what? The Australian illness got me good, eh? (can't recall what illness that was, could be a metaphor). Now there is no time but a good weather considerably improved my feeling of wellbeing. From stone cold tropics right into a scorching hot moderate climate. Despite that these are practically the same climate zones.

It's weird, oh weird.

I think what I meant by this was that I was leaving a country that is considered very hot, in fact even extremely hot, yet, in Sydney during winter, temepratures can drop even down to 0, with an average of 17 as I know. That may still not be considered cold by relatively high humidity can amplify the feeling of cold in many circumstances. In addition, the living conditions, while in fact phenomenal, since everyone was living in enclosed bungalows, it also introduced a feeling of loneliness, as the walls were thick and I hardly have known many people. There were also some other feelings in development, from the perspective of things, I wish they didn't happen, otherwise my exchange might've become a much more pleasant experience. Instead, I had a lot of time and the structure of the course allowed for... well, myself, with whom I did not have clear idea of what I was looking forward to do. It was a feeling of great accomplishment of freedom and... trying to figure out what to do with this freedom and how to ensure that I keep it after the 11 months, hopefully building a better future not just for myself.
