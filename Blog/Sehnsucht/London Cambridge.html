Oki, how do I continue. London? It's a very difficult city to rate. I had to spend some time there and I'm still unsure how to approach it. Is it blasting mad? Maybe. Is it so nice and alike its people? Well, they kinda are. Is it a metropolis? Well, quite definitely but come on. The thing is I've still got Lisbon in the back of my head and alike, Cambridge felt 'more British' than the capital. But then, London actually has its virtues still attached and it's relatively clear which direction it's moving towards.

First of all, all the classics. Gothic architecture of all its ministerial, government operated buildings. Westminster, big ben, buckingham palace. There are some deviations, such as the museums which look more like as if they were built as ancient with all its greek (jonski?) Columns. But then, that's hardly a whiff. The most dominant architecture is the regular brick rows of houses. Perfect for labourers to live at and so the city reminds me of this great industrial revolution remainder, which is, in fact, still dominant especially in its closer (also further?) Suburbs. And then... all the new that surfaces eventually. Actually, not even eventually but kicks it off right in ya eye, once you roll with your bus on one of the estacades guiding you towards the city center. Billboards huge and wide, interactive as if drawn directly from Bradbury's Fahrenheit 451. Near-futuristic architecture of its new buildings, crossover with others in the area, reminding of classic modernism. But it's clear that the city is heading towards not modern but future. With the emblems seducing the drivers to pay for their parking using an app. With the access to metro, being enabled by merely touching your debit card. It's getting increasingly more and more intuitive and seamless and the city kinda reflects in style of how it prefers to grow. Although, it will be years until it proceeds through its entire metamorphosis.

What about Londoners themselves? Well, the accent is ridiculously nice and kind. That's for once and pretty much the majority is able to operate fluently in, let's say, pronunciation dialects, out of which most are so pleasant that it makes me question whether French is nicer at this stage. Classic British. It takes years to acquire such a fluency and it might be the case that how people carry on with their everyday lives actually supports their accent altogether. They are nice. They excuse and sorry everytime even if they scratch you a notch. They get into conversations seamlessly and understand each other even if the pronounciation seems radically different. But all in all? Pleasant people. And London? Hypermulticultural, we're talking of 66% of original Londoners still remaining with the rest having imigrated from elsewhere.
