Zagreb, Croatia


Outside the station

...

Well, my first destination and probably my second trip to Croatia as a whole. Yaaay, I figured, it's time to see the capital at last and as I figured, my first destination was obviously Ljubljana, but then I did some calculations and came up with a solution. Zagreb comes first, I'll have more time afterwards and, well, heh, okay here I go.

Ahh, Zagreb, Zagreb. The funny thing, I easily got inside Croatia but less easily got out, as I was asked for some sort of declaration or something on the border. The officer didn't speak English, but well, anyway, it felt kinda strange and funny how slow things at the border worked and how scrupulous the officers were in checking the identity. But, well, what I remembered and it was the great highways that were already built quite some time ago, but how humongous and high quality they seemed, that remained. But I'm not here to talk about highways and border control, right?


...


Right, Zagreb. I reached the city at.. Was it 6:45? Pitch-black and to be honest, felt maybe not scary, but besetting, enthralling, especially that, again, I did not have cellular and got, despite having a map, by some miracle, lost, by heading south, instead of north. And trust me, it did feel quite eerie, stumbling around and not seeing practically much. Single people around, relative-high traffic and... well yet, still pitch black and feeling as if a stereotypical East European city. Urban Sprawled. Oh I forgot to mention, that Ljubljana was urban sprawled too, but it felt like a nice and well composed sprawl. In Zagreb, I couldn't really tell.

The impressions started to hit a rise once I reached an alley, well maintained, with working mini-fountains. Quite vast, lengthy and actually precipitous with tunnels drilled underneath the roads for people to cross freely. Seemed like an independence square. And the sun was just about to rise so it was perfect. And not that long ago I corrected myself and figured where I actually was.

The interesting thing about Zagreb was, I was quite unsure of how am I supposed to visit it. I started from circling the area because I did not actually know if the city has a main center, google maps didn't really clarify it. There I was, encircling the area and I experienced an interesting history lesson. Well, as alluring as it was, an actual, typical, just straight out of social realism wildest dreams, blockhouses, set one by one in an entire ginormous area. Funny, I didn't expect that, but, ya know. Felt a bit empty, as there were not that many stores around, at all to be honest. But then I headed towards to city center, eventually. And it started changing, growing on me, the city, as it was.

View from the park next to the art Village


...

Then it turned out, there was plentiful to see as multiplicitous signs indicated in key areas. But I still couldn't really figure out the exact location of where I was. I had a wonderful cup of coffee in a kinda stylish hipster place (seriously, the coffee was amazing with all its flavor values and aroma, each cup tasted actually a notch different from the previous one and had an smooth, soothing taste. A notch fruity, then less and.. wow). Had a bit less tasty pastries. And went around, experiencing a densely packed secessionist architecture. Then one of the main squares and stumbled along, what it seemed, as a main road, piercing horizontally the city. I knew there was a vantage point. Oh, did I not mention it? The bloody city actually had an enormous mountain in the background. Felt so... wow.

I did not climb the mountain, but I did reach a vantage point, what it seemed as an old town section of Zagreb. It's funny, how the architecture changes from district to district, it's got a knack to it. The problem was, the sun was rising and the horizon was.. well, the visibility was not that great, all-in-all. Seemed smoggy, but the air felt clear and quite fresh. I took a handful of pics and made my way around the northern part of the city. A combination of hills and... well I landed up soon enough in a recommended by a friend joint, where I ordered a selection of beers and, what it seemed as, meat rolls with a paprika-like dip. A solid selection.

Sculpture in the park


...

Visited a medical campus of University of Zurich. And a cathedral, which seemed as a combination of Notre Dame and Sainte-Chappele. Visited the city markets. And an alley in which many important buildings were kept. Found my way back to the bus station which now, in daylight, seemed quite more welcoming, although still a little worn down.

The thing is, that the city does look quite East European. People are clothed quite alike, in not so vibrant colors. The youth seems as typically East European alike. Transportation system seems quite worn down and what kind of made me thing for a moment is that there is a great gulf between the actual part of the city and the part which attracts the tourists the most, which are significantly better kept and maintained. As if it's being carried by the tourists. And maintained by them. From what I remember, the country seems radically different in the coastal parts, which flourish by the steady influx of tourists. Is Zagreb a good city to visit? Probably, but I believe that Croatia has much more to offer from its coastal parts.
