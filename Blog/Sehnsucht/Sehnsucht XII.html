The Song of Eternity


There were many times when things got particularly bad for me. Things that didn't have to be bad. That were all conceived from the very within of me that were all based on wrong presumptions and ways I perceived life to be. And the very essence of it all was that I didn't really know how life is in the beautiful and magnificent array it is. And that reflected on how I interacted with people and me not knowing how to interact with them really based on everything I have known. And there was really nothing and no one that could really teach any of this to me because... I just simply didn't know.

On one hand it was the opportunity I was dealt that was much too good to be true and as it unravelled as it did, with so much money being directed to me, more than I have ever seen in my life and definitely more than I could potentially spend honestly. All this felt, somehow, just that I didn't deserve any of it. It's like as if I won the lottery and just kept asking myself: why me. How come I get to reap such a reward for exactly... what. What were my achievements. What have I done proper in my life to get to this. Why am I getting this. How should I procede forward is unclear. How should I deal with all this is unclear. Sharing with anyone that burden as I felt seamed just not fair. How do you tell anyone that in a year you'll probably get what I think topped at 50k $ for just simply the act of studying. And how do you tell them that you feel bad about this. How would the others respond? They're also students. They're making probably much less. Some have to work to support themselves. Some make ends meet with what their Erasmus Scholarship provides, for some its not enough to enough fill their rent. Some are on financial support from the government. None of this felt right and there were also some issues that I had to get all of it but once it kept turning into a positive scenario, I just kept feeling even worse about all this. I didn't really prepare any plan or anything to manage any of it all.

This was a pretty good backbone of what I was dealing with, presented with an entire world of possibilities, feared of what if, I mean what if I fuck something up and I have to return some of this money, what if something else happens, what if someone steals something, what if whatever. I mean once I got my bicycle stolen I felt absolutely bad. There was a beautiful silver lining to this but I also felt there was a dire lack of support, I just didn't really know exactly how to keep it all in form and handle the knowledge of all this and keep going forward. It was a mind-boggling frenzy and feeling in fact of as if something particularly bad has happened because I just simply thought I had not deserved any of this, knowing lots of other people out there. And I felt once in a lifetime opportunity like this and I've got a beautiful path of seeing all this wasted, probably despite all my best efforts, which, in the end, sort of became a form of waste. I'm not talking about squandering all the money I received. But just looking back all these years, I hardly remember as much that actually fuels who I am now and what really is of value and what realls just remains, with a few exceptions that are still sort of dissipating in the background, as unfortunate as I feel it all just is.

I guess what I'm saying is just how fleeting life is.

But I associated a great deal with this exchange, I put a tremendous amount of effort to get somewhere and prove myself to be of worth and value and match what I received so I could keep going forward and prove myself to really be of value. And this sort of in years just turned into nothing. And looking back I'm just thinking what could have gone wrong, because not necessarily that much did. I kind of foresaw lots of it. But I still feel bad I couldn't double down on this.

Southern side of where I lived


The Other Dimension

Then there was the way to deal with people. Sadly, all the problems of me not knowing so many thing came haunting me throughout this exchange, despite not being all that all-round bad, still could've been all awash, if only. For instance, my language capabilities. Sure there are lots of exchange students who aren't as competent in English as even I was at the time. But damn it, I'm an exchange student from Australia, I should be. And being in Germany I should have already some decent German skills but I hadn't. And that seriously limited my interaction with other people, as I just felt that, well, even if I talk, what do I talk about, to flow around in conversations between one another. I guess it was the shyness. I guess it was the shame. And I still managed to overcome this to a certain extent. But damn it, it could've been so much better.

And there's the other dimension of me just being a young person. A student, at a respected, in fact, universities and being open-minded and out and about and doing stuff, discovering things, filling your life with everything. Instead, I found my life to be a hole with often not that much to do and wondering what to do and being left to lots of different and not as positive thoughts. And really not even making use of many things that were so close. I mean, I had a swimming pool a few minutes away from my bungalow but I didn't use it, because I barely swam in my life. I barely did any fitness because I don't know how to do it properly. I barely cooked much because I barely know how to cook anything. There's no instrument I can play. There're no sports for me to play if I don't have people to play with and if I'm as shy as I was back then, I wouldn't want to participate anyway. And I still was a bit overweight at the time. Fairly freshly that year I got down 15 kilos, so I was obese at the beginning of that year too. In any case, so many thing out of the books and me just not doing and not committing.

And then's the human aspect. Even if I got acquainted a bit I didn't really commit 100% and didn't take everything I was told seriously, despite I should've. If I did, I would probably even have a part-time job. An in fact I should've looked for one more seriously, because having the experience of a job overseas would've had a lifechanging impact on me. And I decided instead to focus 100% on my studies. Big amiss. And there are so many other things that just didn't work out due to my ineptness to approach them. I realise I'm not the only one but damn how bad it made me feel and how bad it made me feel feeling recurrently about feeling all this. Big broken circle of feelings that I couldn't really remedy for and which I felt were a big blocker for my growth. Another thing that I just felt was a big waste and that I felt could've been prevented if my growing up went differently.

Neuschwanstein


And then the human aspect, again

Not knowing much, not understanding people, not interacting enough and not forming any deeper or even fleeting forms of interaction with others. Because this is the time of life when you should be out and about, meeting people, talking, playing, doing a myriad of things that young people do and me holding back and not really exactly doing all this and not pursuing different things. Learning things. Primarily of all this, just being around people and learning from them and them. Being with them and around them but I guess also having something in return and being able to return something that they'd find of value.

Being more open-minded, being more altruistic and stepping out with suggestions, being more straightforward and seeking out everything, pursuing my interests and what interests at specific moments, not holding back. Making seriously the most of what the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity this was, so that I don't end up feeling like any of this was a waste because it wasn't but there were too many things I just should've dropped as were not worth pursuing. Instead, there were too many nights spent at a half-furnished place that kind of reflected the half-furnished person I was, with much of what I felt, quite in fact true but not that much room that I felt could've had expanded to make it more liveable.

And even as I left this journey quite satisfied with what I managed to accomplish, things turned very fast and in the end I felt at the other end again, an outcast that doesn't have that much to offer again and who can't make much of life as life is because I can't understand so many things because of how just broken so many things in all due process and before were and turned out to be.

It's kind of sad to think about all this in such bleak spectrums but the truth is that lots of this exchange was covered in great deal of shade. Not that anything wasn't done by the books. It's just my life wasn't a great book and it could've been a great deal more, if only.
