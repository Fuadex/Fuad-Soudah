One of the reasons for which I prefer (lately) to travel alone is the ability to discover and to experience the city without any interferences whatsoever (with the slight one of taking pictures, obviously.). On the other hand, travelling together has a number of perks on its own and travelling to a destination where a friend is housed at is even more of a tempting option to discover the city's secrets and so forth.

This time I introduced a combination of all the above. I discovered a relatively small city of Münster, placed a little over 40 minutes up north the Ruhrgebiet, one of the city's you may know - Dortmund.

The thing is, Münster is not much of a big city in comparison with its ruhrgebiet large cousins and as a result of the aforementioned, has quite a few perks itself (that did not surface quite soon, but, well). The city was basically seen in over an hour, as Münster is one of these city's that you may discover pronto, compact city center. Yet one of another german city's, almost entirely razed to the ground by the allies shelling and bombing.


...


What were the highlights? Well, as a city placed near the benelux, obviously a cyclist promenade had to encompass the entire city. Seriously, the bicycles are everywhere and people use it to travel anywhere. Apparently they're like the holy cows on the road, but well, ain't it same in Netherlands?

I was brought to a place (will be named soon), which during lunchtime was vacated, but the day's special proved the place to be worth its fame, for apparently one of the best pastas in the area. Seriously, I didn't think there is much that could be introduced into the pasta business, literally it's one of the easiest meals that in combination you may create. But to bake a green (gnocchi?), with cheese, ham, spiced tomato sauce and additional stuff that made the taste blow me entirely away? Well, that meal certainly has proven me wrong, especially that the interiors were truly mesmerizing (for such places people travel!) And the service was exceptional.

Right next to this place, there was this queer shop with bicycle bells. Lots of 'em. And not that far, basically a palace. Converted into a university, where lectures are usually held. And the city center? Well, it was okay. Quite medieval, a few cathedrals/churches, a bit of protestant/catholic quarrels in history (city's remained mostly catholic). And a river named A. And a lake called Az (?). Which I found to be quite lovely.

The highlight was yet to come though with my hike back to the train station. At 1 am, the area 30 mins from the city center is almost enchanting. Fresh air, wide spaces, beautifully and neatly maintained main street with a bus stop and a dynamically changing time schedule. And on the way back? A truly compelling picture of spacious fields, medium sized villages and a serious of skyscrapers, towering over Münster.

I decided to get off a bus filled with students right next to the city center which was a tuff bet but the one that was worth the risk. This medieval center became absolutely enchanting after hours. Half vacated, half occupied by students. The gavered streets with stones. The architectural parts lit up with golden light. The wow factor was off its limits, although for the most part, an industrial-green mix prevailed, large housed built of bricks that was.

In each place you may find some awesomeness.
