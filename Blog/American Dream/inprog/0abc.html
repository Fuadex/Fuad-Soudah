Then there's the city itself. Where do I start. Well, I'll kick off with my favorite, safety barriers at my local railway crossing. Pedestrian pathways are often too narrow. Which most of the time is not quite an issue at any of the other countries I have been to, pointing out here at Australia or the States, where a pavement are literally blocks of concrete set that often do not measure more than a meter wide. Here in Poland, I just come across cyclists speeding centimeters by you on a pavement which ain't longer than 1.5m wide, at times getting narrower or a bit wider depending on how the people living nearby decided to set their often bricked fences and gates. Leading to cases that if you do use the road to move around, you will often clog the traffic. I normally can maintain about 30 km/h of speed by bike but many will go as slow as pedestrians.
