Framing

What I concluded after these few weeks, is something that I'd love to say startles me. But what it really does, it just makes me sad. When you're presented with a challenge, normally what happens you either take a fight or flight. There is a third option really, a synthesis of both - the creation of illusion. A world of your own. There is even an expression for it, of being delusional but if that's my case, I would have never gotten that far. And I always shared my love for the neutral, pragmatic and fair. But eventually, any values can become illegible.

I went to Europe for a few reasons. First, I started hating being in Australia. I always wondered 'why do I keep coming back to Europe'. Once I showed up, from the many times I've done so, once again, I have answered my question: because I feel good and welcome here. To such a point, that even by my standards I became overwhelmed. I wanted to see my family. I wanted to rest from my studies. I wanted to see my friends... and a girl. That all sounds beautiful, now doesn't it? Now hold my beer.

The balance sheet is as following: I came back sick, with a highly irritated finger, demolished feet, run-down shoulders. My powerbank from KAIST got damaged. My CIEM cable became unusable. The girl doesn't seem to like me anymore. The research report was not reviewed particularly favorably. I was coming back to Australia with a message in my head 'what the fuck just happened'. Because all of the sudden, the extremely warm welcome... I somehow managed to ruin everything. And I'm still not exactly sure how that happened.
