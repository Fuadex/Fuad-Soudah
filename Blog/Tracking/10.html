Readings


Proposal

Eventually I had to filter through relevant readings of papers by Judy Kay and/or Lie Ming Tang. For this, I used Google Scholar which seemed to be ridiculously superior to the native usyd database. At least it pointed me, most of the time, to the right papers, all of which are natively available due to University of Sydney's network. Now that's a good design! I also decided to make use of Endnote, just to make sure I sorted the references properly. Which got messed up slightly in the process but I managed to fix many. Some were not done properly in terms of books for instance of the faculty's release. And this error actually comes from one of Usyd's database links.

Continuing. I decided to break down the idea into layers. First of all, the general idea of ubiquitous/pervasive computing, which I have learned already overseas but it shed some good light on what I'll be researching. Data is everywhere and is being collected by anything. Nowadays, our sofas may be even harvesting data and pushing it towards, well, somewhere. I noticed that some of my writings actually were very simialr to what I read in the papers. Which on one hand was nice, that meant my comprehension aligned with what the academia has to say. On the other, this is exactly why writing a paper should not start with what I know about the subject. But, oh well.

So, pervasive computing came first to give a layer of how data is everywhere and why is it everywhere? In theory, all for the benefit of the user, just so we can build an even more seamless world, hassle-free, good for everyone. Once I pictured this, I wanted to shift towards the microcosm of the macrocosm - Personal Informatics. It is important to know what it is, the context of it, what devices come into play and how it plays a role in our life, and by which means. But do they all mean anything without the human? What about the human? And so I narrowed down on Self-Determination Theory, which gave some very solid overview of what needs to be covered by humans for them to actually want to do something, by extrinsic or even intrisic motivation.
