Chernobyl

What a miniseries! But I also have a problem, as many have called it as the best show ever produced. While I can see why some have named it as such, I must go out in the middle and voice by humble opinion, that it's quite a bit of an overstatement that I just can't exactly agree with.

First of all, I started feeling hyped for the show sometime the show started being advertised, probably early when Game of Thrones was being screened with the exact same trailer popping up at beginning of each Game of Thrones episode. The trailer, was, well mildly put, very good and certainly the hype was peaking on my end. With the show being created as something as if, horrorlike, very much dire and apocalypse-looking in a very compelling way. But as I am experienced with extremely well-put trailers but the shows they advertised for being, mildly put, quite shiite, I awaited with my hopes locked firmly low. At about 3.6 roentgen, not great, not terrible.

Then it came and I was like, yeah, it's pretty good actually. Although I expected something much heavier like the trailers advertised. So I guess I wanted more. Especially, that it was just simply 5 episodes, I wanted more and I felt there was more to be said in the story. The problem I had was already with how it started. How the story was at first so much contained. And then how it all felt so british, like a british Chernobyl which has some good to it, I mean what if it happened in the west? Let's portray how it could have been in the west as well. As genius as it sounds, the show starts to lack a bit with the atmosphere just because of this, obvious actually, decision. Especially that all of it seemed to have been filmed somewhere around the site, which again turned out phenomenal. I don't think the western audience actually has been exposed much to Eastern Europe all-in-all and whenever it was, it was actually quite skewed in ways. Even colorised in ways that made it fake, for instance by portraying Polish people with Russian-speaking actors who did not do well with conveying the Polish. Just an example. Here I could not spot too much falsification happening, all-in-all the details seemed to be at the heart of the show but it did not quite went towards demonising perhaps atom energy as such. It didn't seem to portray perhaps the apocalypse as an apocalypse. It seemed to take a critical stance towards some areas and showcase the progression of what happened drama-style through the lens of what system it happened in.

And again, here there are a few more problems that I had with the show as it overly-dramatized some parts. The dialogue-lines alone felt so American-like, so solemn. The actors have done a fantastic job, though, especially the protagonists. At times it seemed too, again, contained in some parts and some secondary plotlines seemed to diverge a bit from the main story but they also made a good job, in turn, to showcase the extent of the tragedy. I very much liked how the creators reached a bit deeper also into the history of Ukraine and how the cultural parts impacted the entire story. Some points of the show were especially firm here. Perhaps one of the reasons why the audience enjoyed it as much. Still, I felt more could've been introduced and the atmosphere could have benefited from more naturalistic way of leading the show. Perhaps it could have lost a lot of the praise it gained but in the end, I would have welcomed a The Wire version of Chernobyl, instead of, damn, I don't know, any good major movie screened form of Chernobyl, which it in fact just is?

8/10

Well, again, it's fun. It's really fun but what makes it even better is that it's quite well constructed. A combination of a love story entwined with an intrigue, nothing avant-guard here to be found. And so the entire concept concentrates on the action which is truly action-packed, with amazing stunts, fighting scenes and actually all of it. There's a bit of wit and imagination poured into the production and the plot unravels in a rather well-defined manner. There is not much for me to criticize. A few letdowns here and there, perhaps in terms of acting or some details but otherwise - It's tasty, enjoyable and delivers what it was probably originally made for: Enjoyment of crude, rather plain but still a solid action-packed movie. It would seem the one of the more notorious progressive metal bands are back and producing solid music once again.
