Let's take movies and shows on now.

As part of the scanning process, I started to think about what sort of animations I've seen in life and old shows and movies, as I'd see lots of stuff but today I'd remember all this in scratches and shreds. The shows were easier to figure out but movies were a fickle and these I'm still working my way through.

I binged through most of Ghibli movies on Netflix earlier this year and finally understood, from a different perspective, just why they're such a great studio. Except, the general perception is likely that they're sweet and children-friendly but after seeing most of their stuff, this is not main, driving and even dominant quality often. Instead, I'd find often very richly and carefully crafted work with top of the game outstanding attention to detail work, that pertained all across: the quality of animation, worldbuilding, character characterisation, story deeply imbued with references to history, religion, environment, some of which would carry exceptional emotional resonance and some would dazzle with novel animation techniques. Pure craftsmanship. And even if some movies did not blow me away, I'd still see the exceptional craftsmanship that never got lost in any of Ghibli productions.

Of course, not all of their stuff, even if it was said it was on Netflix, in the end it wasn't. I'll get back to this, though.

Then there was the other sort, of short movies. As I suddenly discovered, that there's quite a crowd of those out there. Most of which are... just ok. Kind of surprised me in ways, as I just know how much effort it takes for entire teams to create even a few minutes long short. But among the sea of this stuff, I'd come across some real gems. And I'd also notice some places, which were responsible for those gems, like Filmakademie in Baden Württemberg. And that opened my eyes a bit in terms of some concepts that I've seen elsewhere but were released much earlier. And also just how much technical progress occurred in the last 20 years and more then other things hitting my head.

TV Animation

Eventually I got through a bit too many shorts.

So I got back to movies with Netflix and Amazon Prime at the helm. And those streaming services, I swear - where do I begin.

Perhaps I shouldn't, as there's just too much shit to throw at each. One specialising in producing shit and advertising as original golden shit and the other deciding to list movies and shows for rent all of a sudden, as if to justify the lack of stuff to offer as part of their subscription platform. Not to mention the dramatic drop in quality of most of what they release and focusing on amount of stuff being pushed out instead of quality.

But in the end it all comes down to the problem that Netflix and other streaming services turned into regular cable tv. I walked from tv, because it didn't offer much of a choice. But the more I look at (ex.) netflix the more I feel it doesn't offer much of a choice either. I'm being flashed with lots of covers as if for netflix to demonstrate that there's lots to watch but in case of what really might interest me there isn't much.

And they threw away lots of tools to get people interested as well. There used to be a grading system and they swapped that 'match rate'. At least prime still shows imdb score for a selection of shows! And they really came down hard on vpners, to the point, that they don't even care about their native subscribers - which aligns with my regular cable tv argument. Oh, hey, you're in germany? Would you like to watch some anime? Sure mate but only with german subtitles. It got to the point they don't even show the name of the show but only the cover. Oh, you're in korea and the show's name on the cover is in korean? Well, fuck you, fuck you and your subtitles too. This is just plainly anti-consummer behaviour. Say I'm an exchange student somewhere. Suddenly my subscription is unusuable for most of the offered content. And honestly, I'd be surprised if subscribers in non-english speaking countries are fine with this as well. Say, you want to learn english but all you're offered are subtitled and even dub only in your language. That's just feels wrong, innit?

So perhaps I'm part of the problem as I use a vpn to access shows overseas. It used to be better, I could even connect on my phone. Now it would seem vpn providers have to do all sorts of backend black magic to have those services useable and they're not running in all locations anymore.

And in the end, some locations that do not offer subtitles for non-netflix originals, still somehow pop up. For instance, Ghost in the Shell: Stand Alone Complex had eng subs for 1st season but not for the 2nd. And then when I came back and check, it had for its 2nd season as well. Sometimes I'm out of luck.

But in the end, I kind of end up just scouring through out of curiosity as to what's available overseas. And interesting realisations come up. For instance, in South Korea they have a great selection of Disney movies. In Germany they have lots of really great anime. In the US, they have some animations not to be found anywhere else that are distributed by one very particular, specialising in anime company in new york - G Kids. In Canada they just had a huge collection of Charlie Chaplin movies. Indian netflix is surprisingly powerful. And there are titles, like on Japanese netflix that draw my attention like crazy and then I discover, that, well I can't watch them but they actually do seem in fact very freakin' interesting - and ultimately end up on my to-watch list. Some take me by ultimate surprise and that's when I get the most happy, for some weird reason.

Well, bits of nothing and bits of everything so to speak.

I was making subtle attempts at catching up with the world, as there are certain media formats that I simply feel as if I fell far behind compared to everyone when I account my age and this day and age.

In order to figure this out, though, counter-intuitively to last.fm, which automatically scrobbles everything I've listened to on whatever streaming service or app there may be, I had to manually sort all this out on a website dedicated to sorting this out.

But while accounting music seemed like an easy feat, as I could very easily backtrack what I listened to when I was <13 years old, the very same thing does not carry across for everything else: books, movies, shows, video games and everything. Fuck if I know what I kept my eyes peeled at at the age of 8, 10 or 12, especially if it was cable TV and especially when all the movies I watched were heavily localised in Polish. All I know, I've watched tons.

So with a bit more time at hand, I made strides to see how far behind I was in case of: movies, tv shows and video games on a Polish equivalent of imdb.

And in the end it turned out that... I'm not so much of an illiterate as I deemed myself at first.

In fact, it's quite crazy to think that it's been 1000 movies, about 240 tv shows and about 340 video games. Especially, that I haven't been playing much in my last couple of years and I didn't keep a big tempo with the rest, well, until very recently.

But I wonder a bit whether this all counts and what does it count for.

In case of movies, it's bit deceptive. In theory I've almost doubled the amount of movies I've seen this year compared to the rest of my entire life. How? By bingeing through short movies. Should these count? What about animated shorts?

Who's to say a 6-minute long short has that much of a lesser value compared to a 3-hour long feature? What about a 200-pages long book compared to a 20-pages long one? Or a TV show with over 200 episodes compared to one of just merely 5? Have I really watched a show if I missed out on a season or two?

As I began an educational journey into the world of CG and animation and working in a studio-setting alongside many crazily-talented artists towards an animated short, I kind of realised that I haven't actually really seen that much of animation in last decade or many shorts for that matter.

And as I usually, very counter-intuitively do in life, taking a bite at something without having really that much knowledge or experience beforehand and then frantically doing all possible to get up to speed, I decided to do just as much as that.

And the appetite continuously grew as I ate. Rolling through all the Ghibli movies, old Disney features, Disney and Pixar animated shorts, other school animated shorts, very old animated shorts and old Polish animated shorts. My list kept growing, as I worked my way through all of it, I kept finding titles which for a variety of reasons kept launching me to the moon.

And I could probably list off an entire series of recommendations with all the reasons for why it's worth consuming and what I saw in all this but... in the end there's just so much. And the list of what I deem fit to see does not seem to cease.

...and then there's the matter of what's next.

Well, I could probably just launch Netflix for that matter. But my frustration with this service is just ever-growing, which seems to provide a neverending selection to choose from but under the hood, it's just become another cable TV.
