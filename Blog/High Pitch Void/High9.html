At times I wonder, what is it that I actually want. What is it, that I actually like. In this case to see and experience altogether. I always figured, it's gonna be somewhat a combination, of a robust scenario, that should be as close to being bulletproof as it may with no potential of leakage anywhere whatsoever. And the sound to go in pair, hand in hand to deliver wherever needed, a blast, a feeling of awe, of something that goes perhaps deeper or not quite, suiting the bubble that is being built for its audience. I will also value on top of it all something to pinch your mind, stimulate it and start dwelling on the topics that are broached in a multilateral way. Experimental approaches in all of the aforementioned, if it works well will also trigger a form of 'yes, I want to see more'.

But, in all my honesty, this all sounds rather trivial, an ideal that could be pursued but not necessarily ideal for many other people, who prefer to watch perhaps something else, in a completely different style suiting their lifestyles. Think of, a form of comedy. Like sit-coms. I always leaned towards dramas. But after watching quite a number of shows and episodes that total in, at this point closing in to thousands of hours, I think at some point you start feeling a bit tired with the whole concept, especially of how the form 'ought to' or 'needs to' be executed. And yet, despite the core tends to remain the same, a number of shows still keep popping up that somehow manage to really immerse myself here or there or nowhere. It's even more satisfying to see, that there actually is a show that all of a sudden pops up and kind of checks all of what I am exactly looking for in all lengths actually. And goes beyond of what I yearned for.

I could not have asked for less from giants as David Fincher and Tim Miller, who seemed to have teamed up as executives to produce an animated, anthological series. It felt quite utopian to dream of such an instance, where the guys who know how to make phenomenal productions to be piled with money from a giant, especially of the sorts, Netflix to produce a series, that as they deem as NSFW. The producers engaged some of the best animation studios around the world and once they get a serious budget to produce whatever they wanted, as often as it happens, which it sadly doesn't, when you put the right people together with appropriate resources and no-limit attached, amazing things start to happen.

The thing here is, that every single episode is quite substantially different, one to the other. Think, animation style. The level to which actors were involved in the creation of those. The duration of each episode. The depth of each episode. The boundaries attached to each story. The scaling of each. How it unravels. The dynamism. Universe. Overall quality. The journey, the concepts, the details, the everything literally. It's simply a feast that I have not seen for a very long time. It's just that good. It's quite unbelieveable that Netflix actually poured money into this, as a lot of the concepts involved defy the overall direction in which many of the Netflix-produced shows follow. There is no tokenization happening in terms of the cast. The episodes tend to be crude and involve quite heavily fought nowadays stereotypes. But there's the kind of beauty in all of those, as many of the episodes just feel pure and free of any pressure put on the artists and all involved in the creation of those episodes. Perhaps that's one of the reasons the series did not gain such widespread recognition and press-coverage? All-in-all, you'd figure all you need are industry-grade names. There's a lot of surprises involved with this series and it really makes a pleasant impact overall, despite a few episodes that could have been improved in some lengths. Still, as an anthology it makes quite a wow-effect, especially considering what can already be achieved with modern-days VFX.

Very promising introduction to a rather detailed alternate world, characters being introduced in a proper, unraveling manner, although quite tendentious manner. Then a bit of a letdown as the drama progressed in such bit hollowy formula with the introduction of better and lesser bits, however with it all progressing into more intricate and deeper storyline tanglements and eventually leading further up those without any doubt more interesting bits. With this, it seemed to me that the actors' performance also kicked up and managed to link up, or perhaps it just remained in line the entire time, just the plot didn't have too much earlier to offer but as it did become highly thickening, there was much to be enjoyed from practically almost the entire cast. I think for some it might be too much with much of the yesterday's totalitarian symbolism and I'd even push it to say as even washing out history. But all in all it is an alternate history and there was much to be seen behind the provided frameset, as in the depicted world.

Towards the end, I actually grew quite impressed. I can't still purely recommend this one due to the bland introduction and advancement within this show but it certainly takes off and grows on you, as the world takes on colour with time and many features become stronger with coming episodes. Still, it may be boring at times and too far-fetched at the other, hard to grasp due to quite radical events of which outcomes are too difficult to believe but it all becomes a bit more fruitful eventually, especially once the audience develops some forms of emotional attachment of the showcased characters, which I felt to have been accomplished in this show. Attack on Titan

Season 1

I'd say at times it's remarkable to come across shows which upon its entry, you become so dissatisfied with what you've experienced that you can't help but get back watching and eventually enduring the first painful but then quite increasingly pleasant experiences. That's what happened with Code Geass, which became incredibly good, although still within the very good threshold with season 2 but how it started got all sorts of negative comments spouting right through my keyboard, spamming whomever I felt most sharing my concerns with at the time. And now's the time for Attack on Titan and there are quite a few thoughts strolling through my mind. But I'll start from the beginning.

The problem was.. um, actually there was no problem at the beginning. It rolled in with all its glory actually, beautiful scenery, gorgeous animation, very unique style... well, maybe not that unique but still the dirty blend conceived of medieval and fantasy elements... which one would call just purely might & magic but still as if some fine sort of mythodology implemented. And then going full berserk with no restraints whatsoever, daum it was a ride in which the extremes were definitely not held back. This is something that I do envy from the creators originating from the land of the rising sun, cherry blossoms and mind-melting sushi. They do stretch out the extremes too far though and that did take place with Code Geass, Attack on Titan and pretty much anything that originates from Japan but as always, even in the crowd of badly made decisions, there are some that will strike as something that you have not seen, as in that good, in quite sometime. Attack on Titan didn't really have any of those... with many elements that I disliked.. but it grew.

What did I dislike, though? First of all, the overly cringy characters (oh how I love to remark the cringiness, fuck yeah!). Then the unbelieavable plot lines imbued, with reason but presented beyond except for the effects that took place, which may not make sense at this point. Imagine characters who are driven by a purpose based on an event that took place in their story. The drive is justified by the event but the event itself is just way too far-fetched, not so much to dissolve the joy coming from the show but making it a notch painful watch. Then there are the titans themselves, who look as if they were just parodies or caricature. Perhaps seeing those eating people is something that kicks the sense of what you're perceiving but making a show to the point of being laughable while introducing a horde of elements skewed with seriousness does not serve as much value. Except, it is something novel. And in fact, the all seriousness in the end was presented, deployed very well. And even if there is more that I notch disliked, like diverting action to retrospection or annoying characters, all somewhat became moderate and helped me focus on what was good.

And there was plenty of good, starting with the already mentioned bleak world filled with dirt. But the animation here was something from an almost long-gone dimension. It was so terrific and mesmerising to follow at times with so many details retained that it all just felt fluidly natural. I really enjoyed the world-building and the characters' development. It's a bit like Game of Thrones happening here but frankly, Attack on Titan delivers a much more natural and thought-out batch and drives the story in a way that you can't be sure of who will survive this. In fact, the entire world is made to be very fragile but still complex and I loved how it progressed and uncovered more details with coming episodes. The supporting auditory elements were prevalently stellar. The dynamism kept the show watchable and incredibly entertaining. And even the weaker elements could not mess with how much I enjoyed watching it, especially with the closing episodes. It was a show more worth of an 8 but the way it was ultimately delivered from start to finish, makes it a prime watch and a clear recommendation.

9/10


Season 2

The same cannot be said about the season 2, though. Although, I must mention at this point that it didn't start so badly. In fact, most of the world's structure was retained with the extremely alike styling and characters too. I couldn't say that these two seasons were published in so different times which is a massive praise. So how the fuck did the creators manage to screw it up so badly halfway through? Let's see, new elements were introduced which were great. The progression was clear, the dynamism was there. Technically, all the characters and the story just took on from the first season. New fears and unknowns were introduced and many of the bad stuff was also retained which paradoxically projected for a clear 8/10 continuation. Which even the increasingly apparent defects could not entirely drive off. I counted at least 3 characters who had their personalities, at least slightly altered, as if to make them seem a bit more human. But even more of them became equalised. Normally that could work except Attack on Titan featured highly polarised archetypes which surprisingly worked in this convention, as the emphasis was put on the characters' drives rather than emotions per say. In anime there is a tendency to convey feelings and emotions to the point the viewer could barf. So this is the only explanation for which I could find what happened but this just drove the show to being more tendentious. In fact, many techniques were introduced which were just simply not present before, strengthening the other qualities of the show and its uniqueness but in addition, the show just shows how either the budget was cut or some other people were involved, as the animations also took a quality dive, not integrating so many nuances which I have loved in the first season.

None of it all can compare to what happened mid-way in the season, though by introducing such a twist that it managed to obliterate a lot of what the show was actually good with. This was truly one of the worst twists I have experienced in the recent, at least months. And the way it was further driven... see, the good thing about shows where there are uncovered secrets is that it has good uncovered secrets that remain ambiguous and somewhat freightening. We want to know the answer behind but it's best we did not so that the world remains concise and within a prime set of integrity. This show unfortunately all threw out the window. From which point, everything started to literally just fall apart: the nuances, the details, the atmosphere, the structure and especially world-building and dialogues. You could still see this is Attack on Titan but the joy just evaporated and many structural parts of the show were meddled with, with Titans not being as difficult to address anymore. It's a shame, from an unforgiving and very unique universe, somewhat it all was steered towards... I don't even know to be frank, except I can only express the lows that I have experience and dissatisfaction for the course the what I would call, one of the best anime series has taken.

6/10


Season 3

Once again, the show kicks off properly by introducing a series of powerful schemata. Out of a sudden, the key characters regained their core personalities to a certain extent, which came as a very welcoming sight as finally it would have seemed the show was being restored to its glory. But with that, many flaws came along originating from season 1 and unfortunately 2 as well. To pinch in, there are hardly any titans in Attack on Titans this time. Instead, we receive a western-like blend and new intrigues that pushes on a 'till bit of freshness but also a grand confusion right in the middle of already stirred up clusterfuck with which this show became all thanks to the massive lame twist. We get something similar of grandeur but it doesn't lead anywhere and that's my main issue, especially that many of the newly introduced characters just lack the power that was conceived in season 1. In fact, much of the world's power and fears somehow managed to dissapate along the way, the fragility of the world, the particular bleak style of the show which made it truly unique in the crowd of schematic tv shows, which this title unfortunately attained. It did improve however in some episodes considerably, so you may give it a chance. But I just wish the show's creators could flash with just a bit of consistency because it just doesn't watch well when many elements begin showing contradictory elements defying rather itself and trying to become something that it's just not. In other words, the original had everything, all there's required is follow and concentrate on the world and its progression which is already in place. Once you start meddling with it, nothing interesting comes out, just a pulpy mess.

7/10
