Spider-Man: Into the Spiderverse

This is an example of a phenomenally executed feature film from end to end. It takes under consideration, I'd say everything. The original comics Spiderman is based on but also the movie adaptations. It manages to feature variations of the concept and successfully rebuilds the showcased world based on the variety of world that this movie goes through. The dialogues are rich, animation style is just simply beautiful and detailed, altogether with camerawork (is there actually any? I couldn't tell how exactly this movie was created really). I loved how it portrayed strong cultural references and the feel and dynamism of New York, so that the movie went in breadth and depth, not only in case of Spiderman context but also in terms of the city it was based on (that only New Yorkers will likely get), the backgrounds of the leading characters and of course the context of the main storyline that takes place. It also phenomenally depicts different visual styles, which you'll get once you get your eyes set on the movie.

Which in fact, is a pleasure to watch from the very beginning till end. It's very dynamic and it keeps its dynamism through-on-out. This should not be taken as granted from an animated movie, the creators often resort to a variety of tricks so that they can minimise the amount of work they put, here it seems they didn't care about how much the movie will total in, I mean from the looks of it, it seem like a massive venture, all the way from illustration to animation to VFX to post-production and final touches. Even the special effects here are so imaginative and detailed that I was left in constant awe of just how beautifully created this movie is with an all-out pleasure associated with following the feature.

I also massively enjoyed that the storyline seems relatively bulletproof, with indiscernible twists, very well written characters and their lines, of course the aforementioned visuals but also they implemented some interesting techniques in the animation itself, sound work is top-notch, the multitude of references and just the sole element of paying attention to every single detail that takes place in the movie, making it one of those that you'll enjoy watching over and over. It's an absolute feast to for your mind and eyes and I would highly recommend seeing it if you yet haven't. I mean, this one won an Oscar for an animated movie. And it wholeheartedly deserved it.

9/10

Blade Runner

Ahh... So I finally got to watch the original Blade Runner, after the sequel 2049. It seemed to have received some mixed reviews. I don't know what's that about, I absolutely loved it. I loved a few elements that particularly stood out, the subliminality of it. The entire artistic elevation and all. So that would mean that somewhat similar elements were to be experienced in the original, prompting the sequel to become, what it became?

Well, yes. And yes but yes. So enigmatically described as such, there was one more short created by Sinichiro Watanabe which I recommend everyone to see as it's beautiful for a short (most what Sinichiro creates is beautiful imho). And I think a show is being made based on this too, an animated series which I am extremely looking forward to seeing. I mean, cause why would I not be? It's about a future with artificial intelligence and artificial human beings who look just as we do but are better. We have a dystopia. We have a world in which it seems like a cross between all the countries in the world. A chaotic world, where everyone seems to be adept in a variety of computer systems. A neo-futuristic dystopia that just remains alive but the question remains what it means alive and what it means to be alive in this world, in which nothing seems to give you any hope for a better future. A future, which remains enthralled in darkness all the time and so fixed in the contemporary.

After sprawling a chunk of twisted philosophical terminology, I must say that I was very impressed with the setting and how believable it was overall depicted. The detail put into creating the fictional world and how well put together everything was, the overall design and feel, it's in fact hard to go out and find a second movie like this that showcases such a neo-futuristic (or perhaps should I say, retrofuturistic) dystopia at such depth. Overall the movie feels contained in terms of the storyline it features but it also concentrates on the atmosphere, I feel. There is a lot of art imbued into Blade Runner and perhaps through that lens it should be explored, along with philosophical disputes. The acting is stellar, the overall technical quality of the movie is nothing to be criticised on honestly. It's a captivating feast that explores a potential future we might end up in, with a bit of the corporate, a bit of the social mix and clash and overall dipped quality of living, succumbing into a dystopian chaos that sort of retains its integrity just because it has to.

Well, I don't know, watch it for yourself and dwell by yourself, I don't want to impose my own interpretation. It's like time travel to a different atmosphere, universe and world that only you can decipher really.

9/10

Parasite

I think this was a movie that made quite a bit of a, I don't know actually what it did, except that it won Palm D'Or, a quite massive distinction that a movie creator might ever get. I mean, everyone wants an Oscar. But from where I sit, it would seem Palm D'Ors land into hands of people, who create a bit more interesting storytelling features and step outside of the boundaries that movies are set and contained within. Is Parasite one of these movies?

Well, for the first half of the movie I can set with my utmost certainty, it is not. Although it portrays a very interesting setting and aforesets a strong distinction between people from low and high socioeconomic backgrounds, again, in a unique Korean setting. After visiting the country for a month, I could see a lot of what was in the movie through my own set of lenses. To some extent, at least. The thing is that this movie is primarily a comedy for this first half, a very much grotesque comedy with a strong emphasis on the grotesque-side but still it's kinda directed in a way, that I felt ambivalent about. People in the theatre would laughs at many moments but I could not quite do it along, sure some lines were fantastic but they lacked depth that I was looking for. And so as it turns out with this particular type of works, whether it's in film, video games, literature or wherever, it sometimes happens that the work progresses in a rather plain average if nothing more or less way and then something happens.

This something was kind of structured and put the movie together in a layered way. Suddenly, the movie gains an additional depth that I was looking for. Suddenly, it shifts from being a mere grotesque, comedy into something bit more radically grotesque, splitting its nature into a dichotomous structure in which parallel sides of the movie coexist. And it further adds on complexity with a calamity that reveals a climax. It's like a stomp, the audience does not foresee the unravelling, which really does actually wrap the movie. It's just, we don't see this type of works nowadays, we don't see that much of juggling between styles, that from something trivial shifts to the bleak and heavy, mixing the grotesque and suddenly seeing that a bit too much of the bitter just flooded into the mix, pushing out the sweet and leaving it somewhere, suspended, that we know it's there but overall can't get out of our head what pursued the final mix.

The movie does feel a bit stripped of the deeper venture into the socioeconomic clash, rather remaining by the exploration of how one group takes advantage of the other and how the system comes into the mix. But, with all due honesty, even if this type of movie gets awarded, perhaps it's for the better to give a signal, that we want to see more of the braver approach to cinematography. Perhaps there's nothing that really brave about Parasite but it definitely went down a path that as a whole was ultimately rather quite impressive to follow 'till the end.

8/10
