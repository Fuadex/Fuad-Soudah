The turning point came eventually. The first problem was, that the work was going slow. We'd be in relatively constant touch but that didn't mean that the work we'd be doing would be going ahead efficiently or effectively. We'd come together and think about certain aspects but it would never go ahead as fast unless we did parts of the work individually. This started to shift later, as the idea of the topic of our project was arbitrarily chosen by one of my team members, to which all of us agreed, me, for once, cause I didn't really care about the theme as long as we were going somewhere, which I feel was most of what all of us felt was the direction to go towards. But then, it felt as if I was doing most of the work by keeping mind of all the research methodologies that I have learned in last years. On one hand, drafting the interview scripts that once the interviews were on, my team members would not necessarily stick to and end up asking leading questions. They would also ask questions in a very informal manner that had emotional subtext, which I felt was quite very unprofessional but I could not intervene in the middle of it. The primary problem was and it always was, that in the end I would have to perform an entire transcription and thematic analysis of the thing. Doing this out of a 1.5h interview is not an easy thing to do. And then the analysis itself is also a feat of its own. My team members were not aware of how much time it takes. And in the end when we were doing the affinity diagram, they would source quotes from the work that I have done, which kind of made the entire idea of an affinity diagram void. It became even more concerning from my point of view, as for one of the next interviews, which took an hour, another team member was supposed to be transcibing the interview and thematically analysing it, especially that he didn't attend the interview (because train were running slow, very often he'd say that) but he didn't end up doing it at all. He would also come late to meeting, at times by 40 minutes. So, it did feel as if I was doing much of the work in the end.

Then there was the other team member who was supposed to be doing research. They'd be defensive as hell and I could not really bother with all this, especially they were doing the course for credit and I was not.
