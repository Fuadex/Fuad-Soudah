One thought I had for quite some time now was to get all these RAW photos merged with my Onedrive cloud on PC. The cool thing about Windows and (Personal) Onedrive these days is that all the automatically backed up photos and even all the photos and videos scattered across your cloud can be kept there and Photos app can scan by itself what's on it and display thumbnails without downloading any of it, until you actually select one of them. Couple of issues: cloud, as in singular, you can't connect multiple personal Onedrives - so I had to stick to the 1 TB limit, really painful for me at the time as I had to start moving my RAWs to another drive and do cleaning a while back. Secondly, the photos if opened, at first are pixelated as Photos needs to download them. All in all it's a super convenience but not a perfect one. But what if you add your entire 1.25 TB collection from your drive to Photos folder? And here began magical things to happen.

At first it was beautiful, I looked back very far and I could see amazing things, a complete picture of all my DSLR pictures and everything, really, everything in there. The duplicates were not shown as Photos takes care of this as well. But when I moved back, like really back, I noticed peculiar things happening with the dates.
