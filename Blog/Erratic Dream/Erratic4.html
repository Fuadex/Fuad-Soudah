Not Quite

So last couple of days were filled with quite a heavy load of unexpectedeness. A term, that likely my undergrad program director explores to the verge of existing boundaries and pushing it even further as to make use of it. Let's see. I'm drinking a cup of coffee for 2$ that contains notions of lucreatia, mid-way introduces hints of citrus which eventually blows out into a buequette of liquorice citrus and who knows how it will progress with time. But frankly, the unexpected part was, that I managed to get ahold of a cup of coffee that doesn't taste like a charcoal aka severely burnt BBQ or a cup of dissolved sugarcane. But then as it progressed, as it unraveled and as I discovered in the last few days, that apparently coffee in Korea is considered as black tea and tea itself is not quite as popular, one may begin to wonder of the extent of what exotic really means. On the surface it feels European but deep down, Korea does conceal many secrets.


Excess

Some that exceed in unexpectedness. Some that may seem as extremely interesting. Reaching a boundary of chaos, after which I'm finally beginning to rather giving in. I just passed a pair of girls who have set up a picnic in the middle of KAIST's campus. I just saw a swarm of dragonflies swirling among the campus' highrise structures. This morning, I woke up due to some noises coming from the corridors of my dormitory. As I got up to inspect, I found out there was a pack of kids, speaking loudly between each other. On my way back from the bathroom, I saw an entire class located outside. And I swear, there are no kids located in our dormitory. All this feels like a dream but at some point you just take a halt and accept or tolerate the reality, not knowing what the actual difference between the two really means.

Do you recall me complaining a bit on how Korea was underwhelming? There is the thing that you really need to get to know locals who will show you around and the very best the environment may offer. I was not aware of what the city might behold and it does indeed seem like the neony Japanese style place that one may see in the movies. We just went out on a field trip, one that was packed and I barely got into, as I woke up 10 minutes before departure. That was due to a night out, which was supposed to contain only dinner. That blew out of propotion into drinks that ended up at 4:30 and me now quite remembering the final parts of us arriving at the dormitory. I was being in the process of waking up for around half an hour. And eventually, regretting the decision to actually reach the field trip. But then really enjoying it to the extent that if I did not go out, I would have missed out on a journey, that just backs up all the words that I have aforementioned, especially in relation to the first paragraph.

Ahhh, the coffee concluded with a swift accent of mint. Hooly fookin shiit, this was MINT! You gotta love this coffee. But also the food culture, It's filled with meat and pickled veggies. That I love to some extent but still, I will praise this. Except for when we were constantly spoiled with all you can eat arrangements. So: Lunch, all you can eat... of everything, salads, meats, fruits, coffee... Ice cream even. Vanilla, melon, green tea and... some herby type that was crumbly and my favorite. Although some have laughed me off for getting this one but frankly, the taste was fairly distinct and yet quite tasty. I did my best to get a bit of everything nevertheless. The arrangement in which you are free to eat anything you're keen is something that left me with a wow-effect and all that for around 15000 KRW, amounts to around 15$. This food was perfect for my near-hangover state and would have left me full till the end of the day. But guess what? We had dinner which was held in the very same arrangement... And then the next day, all you can eat breakfast in a... 4 or 5 star hotel was it? Rather traditional lunch and again all you can eat dinner... Oh and the day before, all you can eat dinner was in a shabu shabu style, so like: all you can eat sushi. All you can eat soup with a plate of raw beef to boil... And the day earlier, Korean BBQ what was so mouthwatering and juicy... for around 19000 KRW but the experience... Today, I'm running only on Green Tea, since I'm still full since these few days of baroque-like abundance.

Let's move forward. Now, ever since I went out, I was behaving like a vegetable. On the daytrip. And on the following day, which I could not fully comprehend as to why I was behaving like a veggie. And still, despite it all, the people were surprisingly and interestingly very friendly. Which makes sense, as it is a summer program and people likely will not attend to such arrangements if they're not willing to participate somewhat in exploring the culture. But the beginning are always 'til bit eerie but once the trip took off. Well. Very pleasant surprise. Something everyone was looking forward to. And I just hope I wasn't behaving too strange the entire time. But hey, hangover right?
