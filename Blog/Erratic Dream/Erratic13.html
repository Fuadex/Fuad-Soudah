Haldhi


Well, Here's the thing.

Look, the contemporary cities have some sort of a weird convex happening within. The more they want to become better, the larger pressure they need to apply to get that peak a little higher. I don't know whether this metaphor makes sense, probably not so I'll try to picture it differently: every single city wants to become beautiful, innovative and attract tourists. And so they will continue to modernise themselves, often they would at least. But the way they do it, at times just kind of dodges what they intended as a whole, achieving certain points but really regressing in some lengths dramatically. I could give an example of Sydney, where there are investments done in which what they forgot about was the treeeees. Something I started noticing just recently, how really beautiful this city is but only in some parts and surprisingly just the ones still untouched by the government. In Warsaw they would do something similar at times and put some smaller sets but it doesn't really help or make the city more liveable when it's over 30 degrees outside. Singapore, is different as it's literally invaded by vegetation and the city seems to be actively supporting the continuous transformation of the city towards being even more of a, as they would call it, a garden city. They have a theater which looks like a futuristic sea shell. They have an ArtScience museum which looks like a lotus leaf, although built our of concrete, it does actually have a myriad of lotus leafs around it which also show its beauty midday. They have an amazing, yet another modernist/futuristic helix bridge and others with loads of flowers planted along. Many objects, gorgeously illuminated at night, creating an ambience one of its kind.

At this point I had to start rolling back to the airport, which has proven a bit difficult as I was literally running out of time and I missed my train so I had to make due with a bus. I got in, though. But establishing its route and where I should get off did take a while. On my way back I did find the are quite vacant although still sprawling with amazing, contemporary/futuristic architecture and some of the older, colonial time relicts. The city was very, amazingly clean... And I felt safe there, not as completely as I did while I was in Japan (still my nr. 1) but wow, Singapore was a blast and the wave tore my mind away. I have a few friends who are looking forward to cities recreated in harmony with nature, as if contemporary modern greens. This city has shown me that this vision is actually quite possible, I thought that myself and find it the right way as we don't just want cities, we want liveable cities, the environments shape who we are and how we behave. But there was more in this city which has showcased, that there is much more to it. It's literally a glimpse to the future. If you want to see how it looks like, you gotta visit Singapore. As if a natural utopia, a blend of nature and modern human constructs heading towards the future.

And then? I had to get ready to get going to Athens... and tank a lot of water into myself as the upcoming around 9 hours of flying would make a hit and I needed to survive it.
