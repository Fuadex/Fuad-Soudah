5 Tygodni w Sydney

Additional Information

Australia was always a backup plan, if not the only and primary plan from the very beginning. I mean, just think about it: you have a Polish and an Australian passports. Which one will more likely turn out to be a better document predestining the life you'll be living?

Back in Poland, Australia is perceived as paradise. Back in Asia, Australia is perceived as paradise. Wherever you go, Australia will be perceived as a pradise. As if an Australian Dream but, just alike the American Dream, that became a motive on its own, the Australian one didn't wander that far away from its predecessor's meaning. My idea was simple. Write about what I'd discover here and compare it to how it was back in Poland. But then? My first year in Australia was truly one hell of a rollercoaster, as if I didn't already have enough on my plate. It felt as if, despite so many being pleasant towards me, I made some kind of a truly badly calibrated technical decision making scheme, that turned out for the best, yet my way towards it... Let's just assume, I am quite endurant physically, but mentally? A longstanding exposure for chronic stress? My future with no clear sight? So many elements that could ruin easily even where I was at the time? My life was like a ticking bomb. Somehow it diffused itself, but until then? I even made poetry once. And made an attempt of describing life in Sydney, with seldom success.

All turned out for the best. Nowise, Australia is a Paradise, but for most of y'all, tourists. For people who have to make a living in Sydney, though? Especially for citizens who came back after many years, with not nearly enough in your pocket and not that much to build your life on? Well, it took me a year to finally be able to stand on my feet and I believe that I, despite, might still have got out crippled.

I might continue the series in the near future. In fact, I have quite a bit of material, which is left unpublished. Let's just say, that I publish 5-10% of what I actually write, as it is usual that I lack at times the capacity to finish properly what I have started. Ambition is a dangerous thing. But reluctance just squanders time and that you will not ever be getting back.
