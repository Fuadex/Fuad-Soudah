Certificate in Advanced English

I gotta be frank, I did actually attend an English course at British Council. I considered it a significant waste of money, as I did not get much out of it compared to me just staying in the English-speaking culture, although I did enjoy the learning structure. Especially the combination of tests, it totalled me, a day of drilling through a brains already melted. Just merely after 2 weeks after my High School finals. I was fed up with my mom actually putting me to this, without the provision of any former heads up whatsoever. I was certain, that I would not get as far, as the exam was by far quite harder than what I experienced in my actual finals. Yet, I passed it in seldom flying colors: an average of 72/100? Take a look at the document. In fact, if I didn't get this certificate, nothing that happened afterwards would have taken place, at least so soon. If I received a grade higher by 8 points, I would be considered for a different university. Yet, if I performed worse by a few points, I would not get considered for the course I am in now. Just think of the possibilities?

University of Munich

How I got here? I don't know in the second place. I really wanted to go to Montreal. But I don't know why in the first place. How I survived my first semester here? As a Bachelor, having completed master courses? I don't know in the third place. How did I get so lucky to get myself placed in one of the possibly best student housing complexes, which is the Olympic Village? I don't know in the fourth place. And there is some truth that even follows the magic that I disclosed, which makes my stay here even sweeter than it actually is and definitely more than what I have described. The lectures, the possibility to do my core units on exchange (which I have almost done), the olympic park. Europe itself and that Munich is in the very middle. Cheap flights and high quality of teaching. Close to Warsaw, where most of my friends are located. Seems like paradise, doesn't it? Well, everything comes with a price and the tag I was issued with, contained the requirement of a B1 German Knowledge, for which I had to sprint throughout mere 2 months before submitting all my documents. Making sure that I pass the 3rd semester well was on top of my priorities as well. Yet, once I arrived here, despite all that was handed over to me and how well I was, to sum up, treated. At times I just believe that pearls were casted before the swine. Did I try to match it? Obviously I did. Am I being too hard on myself? Believe me, it's justified. The fact that I was placed here is well beyond even my own comprehension, having done all the other leaps and so on. Still, I believe I have failed, not having done enough as I felt was right.
